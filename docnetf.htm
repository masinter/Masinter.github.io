Documents and the Net

Larry Masinter

PARC/ISTL

November 2, 1995

Outline of talk

  • What is document management
  • Assumptions about the net
  • Document management on the net

Four kinds of applications

What is Document Management

Many components:

  • Authoring & acquisition
  • Information access, text retrieval
  • Library services
  • Workflow

Growing market for products and services for workgroups and enterprises

Assumptions about the Net

  • One network
  • Global authentication
  • Secure communication
  • Copyright management
  • Reliable payment

Four Reasons to Manage Documents

  • Document as Memory
  • Document as Process
  • Document for Product
  • Document as Product

Document as Memory: Knowledge workers

  • Law office:
    • track correspondence, previous work
  • Product organization:
  • manage database of customer complaints
  • Marketing department:
  • competitive profiles

Document as Memory: Opportunities

  • Customer feedback, discussion
    • support forums allow customers to post questions and have answers appear publicly.
  • Distributed workgroups
    • distributed review committee
  • virtual corporations
  • trade associations
  • conference review 

Organizational boundaries blur... Distributed workgroups

Document as Process: Managing work

  • Insurance company
    • claims processing
  • Financial services
    • loan approvals
  • Government offices
    • permits, claims, approvals
  • Commercial organizations
    • order entry, delivery, and collection.

Opportunities: Move process outside organization

  • Order entry: Buying a pizza.
  • Order entry: Reserve a hotel room
  • Order tracking: FedEx

In general, this works when the company can merge the Internet interface with the company's own processes.

Document for Product: Manufacturing and Service

  • Aerospace
    • Every plane is different: repair, specifications
  • Pharmaceuticals
    • New Drug Applications
  • Financial services firm
    • fact sheets about investments
  • Software
    • documentation

Documents for Product: Opportunities

  • Product data sheets
    • semiconductors, plastics or clothing.
  • Product manuals
    • Novell  software documentation
  • Construction
    • VRML rendering

Direct access by customers, communication with suppliers

Document as Product: Publishers and Education

  • Entertainment
  • Textbooks
  • Document assembly
  • Just-in-time production
  • Course pack
    Publishers have a special privilege
    in electronic commerce

Opportunities

  • Promotion
    • information about movies, sample chapters
  • Order entry
    • bookstore on the net
  • Distribution
    • document over the net to point of need
  • Delivery
    • Consumer purchase and take delivery online

Conclusions

  • Taking "Document Management" beyond the enterprise
  • Some opportunities need not wait for technical advances
  • Net is changing every kind of 'document' business.
